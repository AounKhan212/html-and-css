Pakistan

punjab:-

Lahore, the capital of Punjab, is famous for its Mughal architecture and vibrant cultural life. The city is home to the Lahore Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Badshahi Mosque in Lahore is one of the largest and most beautiful mosques in the world. Lahore’s old city is filled with narrow streets, traditional bazaars, and food streets. Faisalabad is known as the Manchester of Pakistan because of its textile industry. The city’s Clock Tower is surrounded by eight bazaars forming a unique pattern. Multan is one of the oldest cities in South Asia, famous for its Sufi shrines. The city produces high-quality mangoes exported worldwide. Rawalpindi serves as a twin city to Islamabad with a bustling commercial atmosphere. Gujranwala is a major center for manufacturing and agriculture. Sialkot is known for its world-class sports goods and surgical instruments. The city exports footballs used in international tournaments like the FIFA World Cup. Bahawalpur boasts historic palaces such as Noor Mahal and Derawar Fort. The Cholistan Desert near Bahawalpur hosts the annual Jeep Rally. Gujrat is known for its pottery and fan manufacturing industry. Sheikhupura features the famous Hiran Minar built by Emperor Jahangir. Jhelum lies along the Jhelum River and has a strong military tradition. Okara is famous for its livestock farms and dairy production. Kasur, the city of the poet Bulleh Shah, is known for its shrines and sweets. Sargodha is called the “City of Eagles” and is a hub for citrus fruit production. Mianwali lies on the bank of the Indus River and connects Punjab to Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.
